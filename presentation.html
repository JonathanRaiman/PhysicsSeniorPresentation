Coupled Mode Theory

\(n\) resonators with complex amplitude, decay \( \Gamma_n\), resonant frequency \(\omega_n\), and coupling coefficient \( \kappa_{mn}\) :

$$\dot{a}_n(t) = i( \omega_n + i\Gamma_n) a_n(t) + \sum_{m \neq n} i \kappa_{nm} a_m (t) + F_n(t).$$

\(\kappa_{mn}\) can be calculated when we know \( \Gamma \) for each coil, through the relation (for two identical coils):

$$\omega_{1,2}' = \omega_0 \pm \sqrt{\kappa^2 - \Gamma^2}.$$


Intuition


  • \( \sqrt{ \Gamma_{source} \Gamma_{receiver} } \approx \) energy lost per cycle.
  • When \( \kappa > \sqrt{ \Gamma_{source} \Gamma_{receiver} } \) tranfer is efficient,
  • More energy transfered than lost per cycle

Conclusions

  • Practical room-distance transfer is possible, but
  • recommended regime is MHz to get \(\sqrt{ \Gamma_{source} \Gamma_{receiver} }\) to be relatively small, so
  • Impedance Matching is tough.

There is hope

  • Geometry and position matter greatly (hexagonal lattice), in fact
  • other geometries show serious promise: pancake coils, chiral materials, (spherical coils?), etc...
